The Problem

Climate Change. Capital C, Climate. Capital C, Change.
It's is a huge deal, and it's easy to forget about. Plastic here, iPhone there.
Undoubtedly, our climate has changed. And it's not for the better. The cause? Humans, and their waste products.


  • Average Surface Temperature Increased By 2 Degrees Fahrenheit.
  • The Oceans Are Warming: 0.3 Degrees Fahrenheit.
  • SHRINKING ICE CAPS: OVER 300 CUBIC KILOMETERS have been lost within a total span of 4 years.
  • Glaciers are retreating. Mountains all over the world are experiencing retreating glaciers and less snow.
  • Many places, such as the US, are receiving less snow fall.
  • We are experiencing shorter winters.
  • OCEAN LEVEL HAS RISEN 8 INCHES over the past 100 years.
  • Climate disasters have increased threefold.


Evidence of Human Cause

There is much evidence depicting that climate change is so bad, because of human cause. It's true.

Since the 1960s, the total carbon in the air has doubled. This is because of human industrialization, the burning of gasoline, and fossil fuels. Also, research has found that deforestation - which we humans have been doing lots of, especially in large forested areas such as the Amazon Rainforest, allow carbon to accumulate in the air by disrupting photosynthesis.

The Solution

There are many things a regular person like you can do:

First, you can support renewable energy sources. Make sure your representatives vote to keep the Clean Power Plan.
Secondly, turn off all lights when not in use. You can also switch to 'greener' light bulbs using less energy.
Next, you can compost some food scraps instead of trashing it.
Lastly, you can use public transportation or an electric car to commute. Public transportation moves many people while using minimal resources. Electric cars often use renewable energy to charge the car, so you can be confident you are solving the climate change problem.
